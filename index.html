Common Scams by Fraudulent Forex Companies

Be cautious of fraudulent forex companies employing deceptive tactics to exploit unsuspecting individuals. One common scam involves a phone call claiming they have exclusive AI software developed by Elon Musk, Jeff Bezos, Bill Gates, Warren Buffet, promising substantial profits without active trading.

The typical scam process includes:

  1. Initial Call: You receive a call promoting an AI trading system claiming massive profits with minimal effort.
  2. Account Setup Assistance: Retention agents offer to 'help' set up your trading account, encouraging you to leave funds in the platform to maximize profits.
  3. Additional Demands: After some time, they may request more money or charge fees, making it difficult to withdraw your funds.

Protect yourself by thoroughly researching any investment opportunity, avoiding unsolicited calls, and verifying the legitimacy of trading platforms.


Beware of Scammers Offering "Recovery Services"

Another prevalent scam involves individuals or organizations claiming to offer "recovery services" to help victims retrieve funds lost in previous scams. While the idea of recovering stolen funds is appealing, it's essential to exercise caution and be aware of potential scams within the recovery industry.

Here are key points to consider:

  • Unsolicited Offers: Be wary of unsolicited emails, calls, or messages promising recovery services. Legitimate recovery firms typically don't reach out to potential clients without a request.
  • Upfront Fees: Scammers often request upfront fees for their recovery services. Legitimate companies typically operate on a contingency basis, charging a percentage of successfully recovered funds.
  • Guaranteed Results: Be cautious of promises that guarantee a specific percentage of funds recovery. Legitimate recovery firms cannot guarantee success due to the complexities of financial investigations.
  • Research: Before engaging with any recovery service, conduct thorough research. Check for reviews, verify their credentials, and ensure they have a transparent process.
  • Consult Authorities: If you've fallen victim to a scam, report the incident to local authorities, the appropriate financial regulators, and law enforcement agencies. They can guide you on the best course of action.

Remember, scammers often target individuals who have already been victims of fraud, exploiting the desire for restitution. Stay vigilant and make informed decisions to protect yourself from potential further harm.

Scammer Script

After studying numerous fraudulent calls, a common script used by scammers has been identified. Victims often receive random calls, and if unanswered, the scammers persist with up to 70 attempts. Unanswered calls are often followed by emails from the attempting agent.

Introduction:

Hello, [name]. I'm [agent's name], calling about your QuantumAI registration. How are you today?

My goal is to help activate your account and answer any questions you have. Please be aware that calls are recorded for protection, and your eligibility to trade needs confirmation for onboarding.

If you agree, we move on to the KYC section (Know Your Customer), covering:

  • Age
  • Experience
  • Occupation
  • Savings, income, properties, and investments
  • US citizenship or other nationalities

The script then varies, asking about your bank, payment methods, account setup, and issuing a security code. Notably, all calls include a pitch portraying banks as using client funds malevolently.

The promoted product is an AI software claiming a success rate of around 90% (a made-up value each time).

The claim of operating in the UK, specifically London, is made. Alleged offices are registered alongside numerous other companies without a physical presence.

After collecting information, a "qualification officer" confirms details, and the agent discusses malevolent banks and the promoted product. It's important to note that individuals lacking perceived potential are often ignored.

Objections:

In response to objections, scammers use specific strategies for retention:

  1. SSL certificates: They talk about certificates, ostensibly from tech giants like Google. In reality, these certificates can be easily acquired.
  2. Comparative reviews: Agents compare reviews of their company favorably against traditional banks, emphasizing lower reviews for the latter.

It's crucial to recognize that regulated companies do not exert undue pressure for deposits. Scammers use various tactics, preying on emotional and mental vulnerabilities to induce victims to deposit funds.
